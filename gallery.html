B H U B A N E S W A R

A visit to temple reflects a synthesis of arts, the ideals of dharma, beliefs, values and the way of life cherished under Hinduism. It is a link between man, deities, and the Universal Puruṣa in a sacred space. It represents the triple-knowledge (trayi-vidya) of the Vedic vision by mapping the relationships between the cosmos (brahmaṇḍa) and the cell (pinda) by a unique plan based on astronomical numbers. Here, at the City of Temple, you get ample religious sites to discover spirituality. Similarly, we need to understand nature and the best possible way to that is visiting zoos and wildlife sancturies to connect ourselves with mother nature. At Bhubaneswar, you get the best chance to visit Nandankanan Zoological Park to do so.


P U R I

Puri is famous for the world famous Shri Jagannath Temple & Longest Golden Beach. It is one of the Dhamas (Holiest of the holy place) out of four Dhamas i.e. Puri, Dwarika, Badrinath & Rameswar, in India. Mahaprabhu Shri Jagannath alomg with sister Devi Subhadra and elder brother Mahaprabhu Shri Balabhadra are being worshipped in Puri (The Purusottama Kshetra). The deities are seated on the Bejeweled Pedestal (Ratna Simhassana).

In the Indian state of Odisha, the city of Puri has a beach known as Puri Beach or the Golden Beach. The Bay of Bengal coast is where it is located. It is renowned for being both a tourist destination and a holy site for Hindus.The beach is the location of the yearly Puri Beach Festival, which is sponsored by the city of Puri and the Indian Ministry of Tourism. , the Eastern Zonal Cultural Center in Kolkata, and the Development Commissioner for Handicrafts.The beach features exhibitions of sand art, including pieces created by internationally acclaimed local artist Sudarshan Pattnaik. The Foundation for Environment Education (FEE), a Danish organisation, presented the renowned "Blue Flag" label to The Golden Beach in Puri on October 11, 2020.
